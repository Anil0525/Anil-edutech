Overview of Banking Exams in India

Banking sector jobs are among the most sought-after positions in India. They offer excellent salary, job security, growth opportunities and social respect. The major banking recruitment exams in India are conducted by the Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS), State Bank of India (SBI), Reserve Bank of India (RBI), and NABARD.

Every year, millions of candidates compete for banking jobs ranging from Clerk (entry level) to Probationary Officer (PO) to Specialist Officer (SO) and even RBI Grade B — the most prestigious banking exam in India.

Complete Syllabus

1. Quantitative Aptitude

Quantitative aptitude for banking exams is more advanced than SSC. Focus areas include:

  • Data Interpretation — Tables, Bar Graphs, Pie Charts, Missing DI, Caselet DI
  • Quadratic Equations and Approximations
  • Number Series — Missing and Wrong number series
  • Simplification and Approximation
  • Arithmetic Topics — Percentage, Profit-Loss, SI-CI, Time-Work, Time-Distance, Averages, Ratios, Mixtures
  • Probability, Permutation and Combination (for PO level)

2. Reasoning Ability

Banking exams have complex reasoning questions. Important topics:

  • Puzzles and Seating Arrangements — Linear, Circular, Floor-based, Box-based
  • Inequality — Direct and Coded
  • Syllogism — Normal and Reverse
  • Blood Relations and Directions
  • Coding-Decoding — New pattern based
  • Input-Output, Data Sufficiency
  • Order and Ranking, Alphanumeric Series

3. English Language

  • Reading Comprehension — 2–3 passages with inferential and vocabulary questions
  • Error Detection and Phrase Replacement
  • Para Jumbles and Sentence Rearrangement
  • Fill in the Blanks — Single and Double fillers
  • Vocabulary — Synonyms, Antonyms, Word Usage in Context
  • Sentence Connectors and Word Swap

4. Banking and Financial Awareness

This section is specific to banking exams and tests knowledge of:

  • Reserve Bank of India — functions, monetary policy, repo rate, reverse repo rate, CRR, SLR
  • Banking Terms — NPA, Basel Norms, NEFT, RTGS, IMPS, UPI
  • Financial Institutions — SEBI, SIDBI, NABARD, NHB, EXIM Bank
  • Government Schemes related to banking and finance
  • Recent banking mergers and acquisitions
  • Union Budget highlights and economic survey data
  • International Organizations — IMF, World Bank, ADB, WTO

RBI Grade B — Premium Banking Exam

RBI Grade B is considered the IAS of banking exams. It offers exceptional pay (around ₹45,000–₹55,000 per month), direct officer-level entry, and the prestige of working for India's central bank.

How to Prepare for Banking Exams

Step 1: Know the Exam and Eligibility

Each banking exam has different eligibility criteria. SBI Clerk requires graduation in any discipline. SBI PO requires a bachelor's degree with at least 60% marks (55% for SC/ST). RBI Grade B requires a minimum of 60% in graduation. Always check the official notification before applying.

Step 2: Master Quantitative Aptitude

Data Interpretation is the most important topic in banking exams. Practice DI sets daily — learn how to calculate percentages quickly, use approximations effectively, and read graphs accurately. Speed is critical: aim to solve each DI set in under 8–10 minutes.

Step 3: Puzzles and Seating Arrangements

Banking exams typically have 3–4 puzzle sets in the reasoning section, accounting for 15–20 marks. Practice at least 5 complex puzzles every day. This single habit can significantly improve your score in the reasoning section.

Step 4: Build Banking Awareness

Read banking and financial news daily. Follow RBI's press releases and circulars. Know the current repo rate, CRR, SLR, and recent policy changes. Read one good current affairs magazine every month and maintain a notes file for quick revision.

Best Books for Banking Exams

  • Quantitative Aptitude: Arun Sharma — Quantitative Aptitude for CAT (for DI), R.S. Aggarwal for basics
  • Reasoning: M.K. Pandey — Analytical Reasoning
  • English: SP Bakshi — Objective General English
  • Banking Awareness: Arihant's Banking Awareness by A.K. Singh
  • Current Affairs: Monthly Bankersadda or Gradeup magazine

🏦 Salary Comparison: SBI Clerk starts at ~₹26,000/month, SBI PO at ~₹52,000/month, and RBI Grade B at ~₹55,000/month — all with excellent perks, housing allowance, and career growth.
