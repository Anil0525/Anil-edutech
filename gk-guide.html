General Knowledge Complete Guide 2026

📅 Updated: May 2026⏱ 8 min read✍️ Anil EduTech

Why GK is the Most Important Section

General Knowledge (GK) or General Awareness is arguably the most important section in any competitive exam. It is the only section where you can answer a question in 10–15 seconds if you know the answer — no calculation, no reasoning required. A well-prepared GK section can compensate for slower performance in Quantitative Aptitude or Reasoning.

In SSC CGL Tier-1, GK carries 50 marks out of 200 — that's 25%. In SBI PO Mains, it carries 40 marks. TSPSC exams have GK questions throughout the paper. Investing time in GK preparation gives excellent returns.

⏱ Time Advantage: A well-prepared candidate can complete the entire 25-question GK section in SSC CGL in just 4–5 minutes, saving 20+ minutes for quantitative aptitude and reasoning.

Indian History — Key Topics

Ancient India

Focus on the Indus Valley Civilization (Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro), Vedic period, Maurya Empire (Chandragupta Maurya, Bindusara, Ashoka), Gupta Empire (the Golden Age), and major dynasties of South India like Chola, Chera, and Pandya.

  • Ashoka's Dhamma and Rock Edicts
  • Gupta period art, science and literature
  • Ajanta, Ellora, Sanchi — Buddhist architecture
  • Important texts — Arthashastra, Indica, Kamasutra

Medieval India

The Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526) and Mughal Empire (1526–1857) are heavily tested. Key topics:

  • Battles of Panipat — 1526, 1556, 1761
  • Akbar's religious policy — Din-i-Ilahi
  • Vijayanagara Empire and the Battle of Talikota
  • Bhakti movement — Kabir, Mirabai, Tukaram, Ramananda
  • Maratha Empire — Shivaji, Peshwas

Modern India (Most Important for Exams)

  • 1857 Revolt — causes, events and aftermath
  • Formation of Indian National Congress (1885)
  • Partition of Bengal (1905) — Swadeshi movement
  • Morley-Minto Reforms, Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms
  • Gandhi's movements — Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, Quit India
  • Simon Commission, Round Table Conferences
  • Government of India Act 1935
  • Cabinet Mission, Mountbatten Plan, Independence 1947

Indian Polity and Constitution

Constitutional Basics

The Indian Constitution, adopted on 26 November 1949 and enforced on 26 January 1950, is the longest written constitution in the world. It originally had 395 Articles, 8 Schedules and 22 Parts. Currently it has 448 Articles and 12 Schedules.

Quick GK — Polity
Q: Who is called the Father of the Indian Constitution?
✅ Dr. B.R. Ambedkar
Quick GK — Polity
Q: Which Article gives the right to move the Supreme Court for enforcement of Fundamental Rights?
✅ Article 32 (called the "Heart and Soul" of the Constitution by Ambedkar)
Quick GK — Polity
Q: What is the minimum age to become the President of India?
✅ 35 years

How to Prepare GK Effectively

  1. Daily newspaper reading (30 min): The Hindu or Indian Express. Focus on national, international news, economy, science and sports sections.
  2. Monthly current affairs magazine: Read a good current affairs magazine and make notes of important events, appointments, and schemes.
  3. Static GK revision: Revise history, polity, geography notes at least once a week.
  4. GK mock tests: Take 20–25 question GK quizzes daily. This improves retention dramatically.
  5. Make an "Important Firsts" list: First President, First Prime Minister, First woman to climb Everest, first ISRO mission — these facts appear repeatedly in exams.

📅 Current Affairs Strategy: Focus on the 6 months preceding the exam. Exam setters typically draw current affairs questions from this period. Events older than 1 year are rarely tested.
