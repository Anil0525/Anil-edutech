Speed Maths Tricks for Competitive Exams 2026

📅 Updated: May 2026⏱ 7 min read✍️ Anil EduTech

Why Speed Maths Matters

In competitive exams like SSC CGL, TSPSC Groups and Banking exams, you have less than 60 seconds per question on average. A student who can mentally calculate 45% of 360 in 5 seconds has a massive advantage over one who takes 30 seconds with pen-and-paper method.

Speed Maths — especially Vedic Mathematics techniques — can reduce your calculation time by 50–70%. Learning these tricks is one of the highest-return investments in your exam preparation.

⚡ Fact: Top scorers in SSC CGL spend less than 40 seconds on average per Quantitative Aptitude question. Speed Maths training is their secret weapon.

Trick 1: Multiplication by 11

✕ Multiply Any 2-Digit Number by 11

Rule: Add the two digits and place the sum in the middle.

AB × 11 = A (A+B) B

Example: 53 × 11 → 5 + 3 = 8 → Answer: 583

Example: 47 × 11 → 4 + 7 = 11 → write 1, carry 1 → Answer: 517

Practice this until it takes less than 3 seconds!

Trick 2: Squares of Numbers Ending in 5

² Square Any Number Ending in 5

Rule: Multiply the first digit by (first digit + 1), then write 25 at the end.

N5² = N×(N+1) followed by 25

35² = 3 × 4 = 12 → Answer: 1225

75² = 7 × 8 = 56 → Answer: 5625

95² = 9 × 10 = 90 → Answer: 9025

Trick 3: Fast Percentage Calculation

% Calculate Percentages in Seconds

Key principle: X% of Y = Y% of X

8% of 50 = 50% of 8 = 4

Use this when one percentage is easier to calculate than the other.

More shortcuts:

  • 10% = divide by 10
  • 5% = half of 10%
  • 1% = divide by 100
  • 15% = 10% + 5%
  • 25% = divide by 4
  • 33.33% ≈ divide by 3

Trick 4: Multiplication Near 100

✕ Multiply Numbers Close to 100
96 × 97 = ?

Step 1: Difference from 100: 96→ -4, 97→ -3

Step 2: Left part: 96 - 3 = 93 (or 97 - 4 = 93)

Step 3: Right part: (-4) × (-3) = 12

Answer: 9312

103 × 104 = ?

Difference: +3 and +4. Left: 103+4 = 107. Right: 3×4 = 12. Answer: 10712

Trick 6: Approximation for Large Calculations

≈ Quick Approximation Method

Most competitive exam questions allow approximation to ±1%. Round numbers smartly.

√(3969) ≈ ?

63² = 3969 exactly. Know squares up to 30 by heart: 16²=256, 17²=289, 18²=324...

Must memorize: Squares 1–30, Cubes 1–15, Square roots of perfect squares up to 400.

Trick 7: Ratio and Proportion Shortcuts

÷ Quick Ratio Calculations

To find a value when ratios are given:

If A:B = 3:5 and A+B = 80, find A and B

Total parts = 3+5 = 8. Value of 1 part = 80÷8 = 10. A = 3×10 = 30, B = 5×10 = 50.

This approach works for any ratio problem and takes under 15 seconds.

Trick 8: Compound Interest Shortcut

💰 CI for 2 Years Made Easy
CI for 2 years = P × r/100 × (2 + r/100)

Or simply: CI = SI + (SI × r / 200)

Example: P=1000, r=10%, 2 years

SI = 200. Extra = 200×10/200 = 10. CI = 210

This is 3x faster than the standard formula.

Daily Practice Plan for Speed Maths

  1. Warm-up (10 min): Mental multiplication of 2-digit numbers × single digit — do 20 problems daily
  2. Percentage practice (10 min): Calculate X% of Y for random values without a calculator
  3. Series completion (10 min): Number series — identify patterns quickly
  4. DI practice (20 min): Solve one full Data Interpretation set with a timer
  5. Mock test (30 min): Full Quantitative Aptitude section mock test with strict time limit
