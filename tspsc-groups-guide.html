TSPSC Groups Complete Preparation Guide 2026

📅 Updated: May 2026 ⏱ 8 min read ✍️ Anil EduTech

What is TSPSC?

The Telangana State Public Service Commission (TSPSC) is the premier government body responsible for recruiting candidates for various Group services in Telangana state. Every year, lakhs of aspirants from Telangana and Andhra Pradesh appear for TSPSC examinations to secure a government job with stability, respect and good salary.

TSPSC conducts exams for Group-1, Group-2, Group-3, Group-4 and various other departmental recruitments. The competition is extremely tough — sometimes over 5 lakh candidates compete for just a few thousand posts.

💡 Pro Tip: Starting your preparation at least 12–18 months before the exam is highly recommended. Consistent daily practice of mock tests dramatically improves your score.

TSPSC Group-1 Overview

TSPSC Group-1 is the most prestigious exam in Telangana state government recruitment. It selects candidates for Deputy Collector, DSP, Commercial Tax Officer and other Class-1 gazetted officer posts.

TSPSC Group-2 Overview

Group-2 examination recruits candidates for Sub-Registrar, Mandal Revenue Officer (MRO), Senior Accountant and other Class-2 posts. This is one of the most popular exams among intermediate and degree holders in Telangana.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Educational Qualification: Any degree from a recognized university
  • Age Limit: 18 to 44 years (relaxation for SC/ST/BC/OBC as per government norms)
  • Nationality: Must be an Indian citizen and resident of Telangana
  • Language: Must have studied Telugu or Urdu as medium or as a subject

Complete Syllabus for TSPSC Groups

1. History of India and Telangana

This section covers ancient, medieval and modern Indian history with special focus on Telangana's history. Important topics include the Telangana movement, Kakatiya dynasty, Nizams of Hyderabad, and the formation of Telangana state in 2014. Questions on national leaders like Mahatma Gandhi, Nehru, Ambedkar and their contributions are frequently asked.

2. Indian Polity and Constitution

The Indian Constitution — its features, fundamental rights, directive principles, amendment procedures, the role of Parliament, State Legislature, Judiciary — forms a major portion of the syllabus. Important topics include the Preamble, Articles 14, 19, 21, 32, 356, and 370. The 73rd and 74th Constitutional Amendments related to Panchayati Raj are very important for TSPSC.

3. Telangana Economy and Development

Questions on Telangana's major industries, agriculture, irrigation projects (Kaleshwaram, Pranahita-Chevella), welfare schemes (Rythu Bandhu, KCR Kit, Aarogyasri), and Telangana's role in national GDP are important. Understanding the state budget, Five Year Plans, and economic indicators like per capita income and HDI is essential.

4. Science and Technology

General science covering basic physics, chemistry and biology is tested. Recent developments in space technology (ISRO missions), defense technology (DRDO), IT and digital India initiatives, biotechnology, and environmental science are covered. Current affairs related to national and international science news are also tested.

5. Mental Ability and Reasoning

This section tests logical reasoning, analytical ability, and quantitative aptitude. Topics include number series, analogies, coding-decoding, blood relations, direction sense, calendar problems, profit and loss, simple and compound interest, percentages and data interpretation.

Best Books for TSPSC Preparation

  • Telangana History: Shankara Rao's Telangana History
  • Indian Polity: M. Laxmikanth — Indian Polity (5th Edition)
  • Economy: Ramesh Singh — Indian Economy
  • General Science: Lucent's General Knowledge
  • Mental Ability: R.S. Aggarwal — A Modern Approach to Verbal & Non-Verbal Reasoning
  • Current Affairs: Monthly magazines + daily newspaper reading

12-Month Study Plan for TSPSC

Months 1–3: Foundation Building

Focus on completing NCERT books for History (Class 6–12), Geography (Class 6–10), and Polity (Class 11). Build a strong base in these core subjects. Read one newspaper daily and note down important current affairs. Spend at least 6–8 hours studying every day.

Months 4–6: Advanced Study

Move to standard reference books. Cover Telangana-specific content thoroughly. Start attempting previous year question papers. Identify your weak areas and give them extra attention. Begin solving sectional mock tests.

Months 7–9: Revision and Practice

Start full-length mock tests at least 3 times per week. Analyze your performance after each test — identify mistakes and revise those topics. Prepare short notes for quick revision. Group study can be helpful in this phase.

Months 10–12: Final Preparation

Take daily mock tests. Focus on time management — practice completing the paper within the given time. Revise current affairs from the last 12 months. Stay calm, maintain a healthy routine, and trust your preparation.

🎯 Key Strategy: Students who practice 10+ full-length mock tests before the exam perform significantly better. Regular practice builds speed, accuracy and exam confidence.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Ignoring Telangana-specific topics: Many candidates focus only on general studies and neglect Telangana history, geography and current affairs — which carry heavy weightage in TSPSC exams.
  2. Not practicing mock tests: Reading without practice is insufficient. Mock tests help you understand the exam pattern and improve time management.
  3. Poor time management during the exam: Don't spend too much time on a single question. Move forward and come back to difficult questions at the end.
  4. Ignoring current affairs: TSPSC exams always include 15–25 questions on current events. Read the newspaper daily.
  5. Starting too late: TSPSC exams are highly competitive. Start early and be consistent.
